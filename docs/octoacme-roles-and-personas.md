# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define and execute quality assurance strategies, ensuring features meet acceptance criteria and maintain quality standards. They collaborate with product and development teams to establish testing approaches and validate readiness for release.

### Responsibilities
- Define test plans and acceptance criteria validation approach
- Design and maintain automated and manual test cases
- Identify quality risks and gaps in coverage
- Coordinate testing across environments (staging, production)
- Report on test results and quality metrics
- Escalate blockers and critical defects

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Catch defects early to reduce rework
- Maintain consistent quality standards across releases

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria refinement
- Daily standups on test progress and blockers
- Quality reports in weekly syncs

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters or Agile Coaches remove process blockers, facilitate team ceremonies, and coach teams on continuous improvement. They ensure the team adheres to agreed processes and creates psychological safety for feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, retrospectives, and standups
- Remove impediments blocking team progress
- Coach team on Agile practices and process adherence
- Track team health metrics (velocity, burndown, morale)
- Identify and escalate systemic process issues

### Goals
- Enable consistent, predictable delivery cadence
- Foster team collaboration and psychological safety
- Improve process efficiency through iterative refinement

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies facilitation
- 1:1 coaching with team members
- Retrospective insights shared with leadership

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide business context, secure resources, and make decisions on trade-offs and priorities. They represent business interests and ensure project alignment with organizational strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Define business needs and success criteria
- Approve scope, timeline, and resource allocation
- Make trade-off decisions when conflicts arise
- Provide regular feedback and validate assumptions
- Communicate project status to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers measurable business value
- Enable cross-functional alignment and buy-in
- Support timely decision-making and risk mitigation

### Typical Communication
- Milestone and monthly stakeholder updates
- Decision gates and approval meetings
- Escalation of business-impacting risks

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects define solution architecture, evaluate technology choices, and ensure technical decisions align with long-term strategy. They provide technical guidance and identify architectural risks.

### Responsibilities
- Define system architecture and technology strategy
- Evaluate and recommend technology choices
- Review designs for scalability, security, and maintainability
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Mentor developers on architectural patterns

### Goals
- Ensure scalable, maintainable, and secure technical solutions
- Reduce technical debt and architectural rework
- Align technology decisions with organizational strategy

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and architecture discussions
- Technical risk assessment in planning
- Guidance during development blockers

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployments, ensure production readiness, and manage release communication. They own the release checklist, deployment sequencing, and post-release verification.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release checklists and procedures
- Coordinate deployment sequencing and timing
- Verify pre-release requirements are met
- Conduct post-deployment smoke tests and verification
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Manage rollback procedures if issues arise

### Goals
- Ensure smooth, low-risk deployments to production
- Minimize downtime and post-release issues
- Maintain clear release communication and transparency

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release coordination meetings
- Release notes and deployment updates
- Post-deployment verification and incident communication
